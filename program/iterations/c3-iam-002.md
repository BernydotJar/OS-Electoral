## Delivered behavior

- Reversible Alembic revision `20260721_0004` adds tenant-owned invitation, application-session and support-access records plus membership versions under forced RLS.
- Invitation create is exact-authorized, purpose-bound and idempotent; it writes audit, internal no-effect outbox and replay evidence atomically.
- Acceptance requires a cryptographically verified principal with matching normalized email and `email_verified=true`; it is one-time and creates no implicit role or grant.
- Invitation revocation and membership revocation enforce exact resource/action/purpose authority and optimistic versions.
- Application sessions store only a provider-session digest, persist expiry/revocation and support audited self or explicitly authorized cross-principal revocation.
- Support requests bind target, tenant, campaign/workspace, action, resource, purpose, reason, expiry and requester. Approval enforces separation of duty and creates only expiring exact authority.
- Support revoke/expiry disables the exact grant and support role. A lifecycle-created membership is disabled; a pre-existing membership and unrelated authority remain active.
- Repeated support cycles preserve request, role and audit history and cannot create duplicate active authority.
- Local and Cognito invitation planners are request plans only; no external provider adapter is invoked.
- API routes publish typed OpenAPI and sanitize denial, conflict, stale version, missing resource and dependency failures.

## Critic findings repaired

1. Expiry helpers existed without direct persistence/replacement evidence. Added invitation, session and support expiry regressions.
2. Email equality alone did not prove provider verification. OIDC claim validation now preserves `email_verified`, and invitation acceptance requires `true`.
3. Redundant scope keys could drift from campaign/workspace UUIDs. Added database check constraints and corruption regressions.
4. Support revoke lacked a direct proof that unrelated access survives. Added a pre-existing membership/role/grant preservation test.
5. The new domain reduced repository coverage to `87.35%`. Added adversarial state, rollback and error-sanitization tests; the unchanged 90% floor now passes at `91.34%`.
6. The original support regrant approach risked lossy migration semantics. Regrant reuses only lifecycle-owned exact authority while preserving append-only request/role/audit history.
