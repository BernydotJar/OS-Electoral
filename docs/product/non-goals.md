# CampaignOS non-goals

Status: **PROPOSED**
Last updated: `2026-07-19`

CampaignOS is not intended to be:

- a voter-scoring, political-surveillance, persuasion-optimization, or psychological-targeting platform;
- an autonomous political actor, publisher, ad buyer, field organizer, fundraiser, or citizen-contact system;
- a substitute for qualified legal, political-science, sociological, anthropological, psychological, research, security, privacy, accessibility, or election-authority review;
- a public mandate detector or a mechanism for converting engagement metrics into claims of democratic consent;
- an identity provider or a system where OIDC claims alone grant tenant or campaign authorization;
- a general-purpose CRM for unconsented political contact data;
- a source of fabricated candidate attributes, evidence, citations, public sentiment, or opposition claims;
- a covert employee or volunteer monitoring system;
- a campaign–government data bridge;
- a real-time social-media manipulation or coordinated influence engine;
- a microservice or Kubernetes platform in the initial release;
- a billing or payment-processing platform in the first enterprise pilot;
- production-ready merely because unit tests, a static demo, a container build, or one environment succeeds.

Potential future scope requires a reviewed ADR, product-boundary update, threat/privacy analysis, hard evals, and authorized human approval. No future scope may weaken the hard prohibitions.
